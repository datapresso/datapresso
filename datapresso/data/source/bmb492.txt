Boston Marathon bombing: Three more suspects arrested twin blasts that killed three people and injured more than 260 	 		 		 					 			 	 	  		 								 			 A further three people have been arrested in connection with the Boston Marathon bombings, it was revealed today. After making an initial announcement via Twitter, Boston Police Department confirmed the arrests had been made but referred all further inquiries to the FBI. They added that more information on the arrests would be revealed later today. Three people were killed and more than 260 injured on April 15, when two homemade bombs exploded close to the marathon’s finish line. After one of the largest manhunts in US history, suspect Tamerlan Tsarnaev died during a gunfight with police. His brother, Dzhokhar Tsarnaev, was captured days later and remains in a prison hospital.
